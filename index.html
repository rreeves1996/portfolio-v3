About Me

If I was asked to describe myself, I would say my identity is that of a lifelong musician, learner, and oxford comma user.

Being born in 1996, I was fortunate enough to witness first-hand the monumental advancements we have made towards technology and computers the past 26 years - advancements that completely altered our everyday lives.

As I navigated through my adolescence, I became more and more intrigued about combining my passion for the arts and creative expression with technology. I dove head-first in to the oddly green-ish, uncomfortably lukewarm waters of graphic design as got older, which completely broke my brain. Since then - for over a decade, now - my stubborn brain has picked apart every website I've visited and every app I've used. My early and late teenage years would consist of me using my abundant amount of annoying, youthful energy to scour the nuances that made certain websites or apps just "feel" or look better as a whole.

I started programming in my teenage years, attending video game development camps. Although I loved video games (and still do), my interest eventually strayed away from programming them, despite my interest in video games overall never waivering (don't look at my steam account value). What really intrigued me was the creative, often stupid ways I could get something to work, or the frustrating issues I would have that would often reveal curious details about how the code worked. Despite this, I would find myself in the trades for the majority of my career past high school - first as an automotive technician, then in brick and stone masonry, where I reside today.

Recently, after a lot of deliberation, I begun to question my career path for various reasons. Although I enjoyed working with my hands, learning something new every day, and being a part of teams that were so closely-knit that they felt like family, I decided it was in my best interest in the long run to pursue a career in software development and design. It is almost overwhelmingly exciting to know that I have only scratched the surface of design and development, and I can't express how grateful I am to be fortunate enough to have the option to switch in to a new career where I know I will excel!

What is my current focus?

Currently, I am looking for ways to further my education/skills in front-end development and design! I have thoroughly enjoyed any front-end oriented work I've done so far, and it excites me to know that I am only in the beginning stages of my learning.
